# THE COMMONS

**Human Experience, Held in Common.**

THE COMMONS is an emerging governance and interoperability framework
for human experience entering digital and AI systems.

It explores how lived experience can travel with consent, provenance,
cultural context, control, correction rights, and reciprocity.

> AI governance is also the governance of human experience.

## Current Status

**Version 0.1 · 2026**  
**Public Draft for Discussion and Pilot Testing**

This is not a finished standard.

It is being published to identify where the architecture may fail,
reproduce extraction, centralize power, or misrepresent people and cultures.

## Core Principles

- **Consent** — Openness begins with meaningful consent.
- **Provenance** — Source remains attached to content.
- **Context** — Meaning must not be separated from its circumstances.
- **Control** — Permissions may be limited, revised, withdrawn, or refused.
- **Reciprocity** — Recognition and value should return to the source.

## Core Prototype: Experience Packet

An Experience Packet is a portable unit of lived experience bundled with:

- experience content
- contributor information
- provenance
- cultural context
- permissions
- AI-processing history
- attribution requirements
- correction status
- withdrawal status
- derivative relations
- reciprocity records

One permission does not authorize every use.

Public display, research, translation, AI analysis, generation,
model training, and commercial use must be governed separately.

## Founding Practice

**Youth Culture Exchange** is the founding practice initiative of THE COMMONS.

It began through poetry, photography, urban fieldwork, and the attempt
to preserve ordinary human experience before it disappeared.
